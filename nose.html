Spurs coach Gregg Popovich calls Cowboys owner Jerry Jones ‘hypocritical’ for past support of Donald Trump

Popovich joins 49ers CB Richard Sherman as a recent sports figure to publicly criticize Jones.

By SportsDay Staff

10:28 PM on Jun 14, 2020

San Antonio Spurs head coach Gregg Popovich hasn’t been shy about criticizing President Donald Trump publicly.

He also wasn’t shy about calling out Cowboys owner Jerry Jones, Patriots owner Robert Kraft and several NFL owners who have supported Trump’s Inaugural Committee.

“It’s just hypocritical,” Popovich said in an interview with The New York Times. “It’s incongruent. It doesn’t make sense. People aren’t blind. Do you go to your staff and your players and talk about injustices and democracy and how to protest? I don’t get it. I think they put themselves in a position that’s untenable.”

More than 12% of Trump’s $106.7 million inauguration fundraising effort came from Texas, with $1 million coming from Glenstone Limited Partnership, a group that shares an address with the franchise’s Frisco headquarters and has state filings that show direct connections to Jones.

San Francisco 49ers cornerback Richard Sherman also criticized Jones recently, but for a different reason.

While the NFL and many of its teams and players have made public statements about the death of George Floyd, Jones has been silent. That didn’t sit right with Sherman.

“It’s not pulling [NFL owners] like it is the rest of the country,” Sherman said, according to Eric Branch of the San Francisco Chronicle. “Because if it was, then they’d speak. Jerry Jones, especially, has no problem speaking up any other time about anything else. But when it’s such a serious issue, and he could really make a huge impact on it with a few words, his silence speaks volumes.”

NFL players such as Cowboys running back Ezekiel Elliott and Kansas City Chiefs quarterback Patrick Mahomes banded together to create a video to implore the league to condemn racism, admit it was wrong to silence earlier protests and to say, “black lives matter.’’

Commissioner Roger Goodell responded by admitting the NFL was wrong for not listening to its players earlier and encouraging all to speak out and peacefully protest.

“It has been a difficult time for our country. In particular, black people in our country,” Goodell said. “First, my condolences to the families of George Floyd, Breonna Taylor, Ahmaud Arbery and all the families who have endured police brutality. We, the National Football League, condemn racism and the systematic oppression of black people. We, the National Football League, admit we were wrong for not listening to NFL players earlier and encourage all to speak out and peacefully protest. We, the National Football League, believe Black Lives Matter. I personally protest with you and want to be part of the much-needed change in this country."

Popovich had a response for the commissioner as well.

“A smart man is running the N.F.L. and he didn’t understand the difference between the flag and what makes the country great — all the people who fought to allow [Colin] Kaepernick to have the right to kneel for justice," he said. "The flag is irrelevant. It’s just a symbol that people glom onto for political reasons, just like Cheney back in the Iraq war... He got intimidated when Trump jumped on the kneeling...

"He folded.”
